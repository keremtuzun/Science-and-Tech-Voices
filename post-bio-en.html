Engineering Living Systems

For example, thanks to CRISPR-Cas9 technology, scientists can now edit DNA almost like computer code. From Cell to Chip: Bio-Integrated Technologies

Advances in nanotechnology are bridging the gap between biology and the physical world. Researchers are developing biosensor chips that connect signals from living cells to electronic systems. These chips have potential applications ranging from health monitoring to environmental analysis.

Such systems make it possible to analyze biological data from the human body in real time, paving the way for personalized medical solutions. In doing so, the human body becomes part of a dynamic ecosystem that can directly interact with technology.
